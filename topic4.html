Advancing Urban Development through Research: Establishing Innovative Solutions for Sustainable Cities

Theorizing Spatial Justice and Urban Mobility

by Anton Hinrich Rennesland

Prof. Anton Hinrich Rennesland talked about the idea of spatial justice, which means ensuring fairness in how spaces and transportation systems are planned and used in cities. He explained that many people experience inequality because some areas have more access to transportation, safer roads, and better mobility options than others. Research helps uncover these issues and guides urban planners in creating solutions that benefit everyone.

He also highlighted that improving urban mobility is important for building fair and sustainable cities. When public transportation, walkways, and bike lanes are designed well, people can move safely and efficiently regardless of where they live. He also showed that spatial justice is not only about transportation but also about giving all communities equal opportunities to access schools, jobs, and services. His talk reminded listeners that cities become better when mobility systems treat everyone fairly.
