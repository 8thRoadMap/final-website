Advancing Urban Development through Research: Establishing Innovative Solutions for Sustainable Cities

Educating for Sustainable Cities Through Design Thinking

by Dr. Joelash Honra

Dr. Joelash R. Honra, an Associate Professor at the National University, Philippines, delivered a compelling presentation on the role of education in building sustainable cities. His study emphasized that schools have a responsibility not just to teach academic knowledge, but to help students develop the skills needed to address real-life problems in their communities.

He highlighted that competencies such as problem-solving, teamwork, empathy, and creative thinking are essential for preparing young people to become active contributors to society. By allowing students to engage in real projects and community-based activities, schools can equip them with the ability to tackle complex issues and understand the impact of their work on the world around them.

‎Dr. Honra also introduced the concept of Design Thinking as a framework that can transform how students approach challenges. He explained that education plays a crucial role in nurturing a person’s mindset, it shapes their ability to think critically, innovate responsibly, and collaborate for social good. Developing sustainable thinkers, he argued, is the foundation of creating sustainable cities. When students are trained to be empathetic, creative, and solution-oriented, they are more likely to propose meaningful, human-centered solutions to urban problems.
