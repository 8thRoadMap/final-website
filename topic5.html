Advancing Urban Development through Research: Establishing Innovative Solutions for Sustainable Cities

Advancing Urban Development Through Research: Establishing Innovative Solutions for Sustainable Cities

by Bambang Brodjonegoro

Dean Bambang Brodjonegoro opened the first day of the congress with a presentation that centered on population growth, urban management, and the escalating challenges posed by climate change. He emphasized that rapid urbanization is reshaping cities at an unprecedented pace, projecting that by 2050, around 68% of the country’s population will be living in urban areas. Such a dramatic shift, he explained, demands proactive planning and the implementation of forward-looking policies.

‎He warned that continuing to rely on outdated infrastructure and traditional planning approaches will leave cities unprepared for emerging problems. To prevent this, he introduced data-driven strategies that can help local governments anticipate future needs, allocate their resources efficiently, and avoid small issues from growing into major crises.

‎In addition to demographic projections, Dean Brodjonegoro also highlighted the vital role of sustainability in urban development. He emphasized that cities must adapt not only to population growth but also to changing environmental conditions brought on by climate change. As part of his recommendations, he encouraged city governments to adopt renewable energy technologies such as rooftop solar panels and wind power, solutions that could supply sustainable, long-term energy while reducing environmental impact.
