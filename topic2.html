Advancing Urban Development through Research: Establishing Innovative Solutions for Sustainable Cities

Innovative Solutions in Contemporary Healthcare

‎by Shuaibu Saidu Musa

‎Prof. Shuaibu Saidu Musa, a researcher from Chulalongkorn University in Bangkok, Thailand and a faculty member of the College of Medicine, was one of the presenters who examined current health innovations during the congress. His presentation focused on the growing integration of technology in modern healthcare, emphasizing tools such as telemedicine, AI-assisted diagnostics, and wearable health devices.

‎He explained that these innovations have the potential to significantly improve healthcare access, especially for underserved populations. However, he also noted that these technologies can only reach their full impact if social and economic contexts are considered. Issues such as affordability, infrastructure limitations, and unequal access must be addressed so that technological advancements translate into practical benefits for a wider population.

‎In addition to presenting emerging technologies, Prof. Musa highlighted real-world examples of student-led innovations, such as the work of Muhammad Astaf Ahmed, who developed a website capable of identifying possible illnesses based on reported symptoms. While these innovations demonstrate great promise, he acknowledged that several barriers, including regulatory constraints, data privacy concerns, and the high cost of medical technologies, continue to challenge their widespread adoption.

‎Despite these hurdles, Prof. Musa emphasized the crucial role of universities in shaping future healthcare leaders, particularly by fostering critical thinking, research skills, and creativity among their students.
